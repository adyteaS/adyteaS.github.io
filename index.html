About Me

I am a Research Data Scientist at Cedars-Sinai Health System in Los Angeles, where I work on developing foundation models for cancer biology and drug toxicity prediction at the single cell level.

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, computational biology, and drug discovery. I specialize in:

  • Foundation Models: Developing and applying large-scale AI models for translational cancer biology
  • Single Cell Genomics: Predicting drug toxicity and cellular responses using single cell RNA sequencing data
  • Machine Learning for Drug Discovery: Extracting drug-gene interactions and toxicity models using LLM approaches
  • Multi-omic Integration: Combining diverse biological data types for comprehensive insights

Current Research

At Cedars-Sinai, I'm leading a novel single cell project that predicts drug toxicity at the cellular level using advanced computational methods. I'm also developing innovative foundation models that integrate LLM approaches to analyze gene expression data across multiple conditions, with the goal of revealing new drug toxicity interactions in single cell space.

I recently co-authored a comprehensive foundation model review paper published in the Annual Review of Biomedical Data Science (2025).

Background

Prior to joining Cedars-Sinai, I worked as a Bioinformatics Data Analyst at Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center, where I:

  • Led single cell RNAseq analysis for novel drug development projects
  • Built high-accuracy deep learning models for cancer genomics (100+ GB datasets)
  • Improved liver cancer classifier accuracy by 20%
  • Mentored 6 interns and trained PhD students in single cell omics and ML

I hold an M.S. in Computer Science (Machine Learning) from the University of New Orleans and a B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, India.
